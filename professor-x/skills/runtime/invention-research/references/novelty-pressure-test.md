# Novelty Pressure Test

Use this when a candidate idea starts sounding new.

## First-pass kill questions

- Is this just a known theory with renamed parts?
- Is this already implemented in a repo or architecture paper?
- Is the "novelty" only the combination, with no new mechanism?
- Is the measurement already standard in another nearby field?
- Is the claim relying on a weak or outdated view of the literature?

## Closest-overlap test

For the top 3-5 closest overlaps, state:

- what they already do
- where they stop
- what your candidate adds
- whether that addition is structural or only rhetorical

If the addition is rhetorical, kill the claim.

## Repo test

Check not just papers but code. A repo that already operationalizes the core
move can kill a novelty claim even if the paper framing differs.

## Survival standard

A candidate survives only if you can say:

"These systems cover A, B, and part of C. The remaining gap is D, and D is the
actual claim."
