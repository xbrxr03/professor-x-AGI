# Plan: reach 11/10 quality

**11/10 = a self-improvement curve that bends upward over multiple turns, on a LOCAL model,
eventually on real usage.** Not one accepted gate. The deliverable is a *line going up*, measured
honestly. Nothing counts until the ruler (repo-fix pass@1, K-pass averaged, > MDE) says so — this is
the M0 discipline and it is non-negotiable (this project has shipped a fabricated "rise" before).

Current standing (2026-06-17): ~40–50% toward the north star. The structural half (harness parity +
a trustworthy benchmark) is largely done; the defining half (capability high enough that people use
it + a self-improvement curve) is mostly ahead. As a plain local coding agent: ~7/10. As an 11/10
self-improving system: not there — M4 has one unfinished turn to its name.

See also [MILESTONE.md](MILESTONE.md). Principle that governs this plan: **drive one vertical, do
not build breadth.**

---

## Phase 1 — Prove the flywheel *turns* (M4 pulse) — now → ~1 week
The distillation flywheel turn running now is turn 1. One turn is not a curve.
- If the gate ACCEPTS: re-run from the new, higher baseline. Repeat.
- If it REJECTS: diagnose from the trajectory (never guess — trajectory-diagnosed fixes lifted
  repo-fix 0.50→0.77). Knobs: `PX_EPOCHS=2`, more teacher passes, harder corpus.
- **Exit gate:** ≥3 consecutive turns, each beating the prior baseline by > MDE (0.05), K=3 averaged.

## Phase 2 — Raise the ceiling, not just the floor (M2) — parallel with Phase 1
0.857 on 14 tasks is too easy a ruler to reach 11/10 against; a low bar saturates the flywheel fast.
- Grow repo-fix to 50–100 harder, multi-file, representative bugs. Every fixture validated red→green
  with stdlib checks (NOT pytest — it is not installed) per the `add-repofix-fixture` rule.
- Promote the teacher to qwen2.5-coder:32b (already pulled) for a harder solved-frontier.
- **Exit gate:** a bigger benchmark with a re-pinned, honest baseline and real headroom to climb.

## Phase 3 — Close the autonomy loop (M4 structural safety) — ~2 weeks
Self-improving means *it* runs unattended and keeps only gated wins — not me running a script.
- Wire the 5 structural safety rails: deny-list, pinned-eval, full-test, reward-scan, hold-out.
  Safety is structural, not human-in-the-loop.
- Schedule turns automatically; auto-accept only past-gate improvements; auto-rollback the rest.
- **Exit gate:** a curve produced with no human touching it — the artifact that proves M4.

## Phase 4 — Ship M3 so it improves on *real* usage — the 11/10 endgame
Synthetic fixtures plateau. The unique thing nobody else has is improving on what people actually do
offline.
- Ship the frictionless local launch (profx TUI / `--serve` / `@file`).
- Pipe real-usage trajectories into the same gated flywheel.
- **Exit gate:** the curve keeps bending up on real tasks, not just the benchmark. That is 11/10.

---

## Two honest risks this plan must beat
1. **Corpus saturation** — once the student catches the teacher, headroom dies. Phase 2's
   frontier-growing fixtures and Phase 4's real tasks are the antidote; not optional.
2. **Benchmark overfit** — a rising number on a frozen 14-task set is a mirage. The Phase 3 hold-out
   and Phase 4 real usage are what keep the ruler honest.
