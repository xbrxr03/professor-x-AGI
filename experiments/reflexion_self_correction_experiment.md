## Reflexion Self-Correction Experiment

### Objective
Measure whether Reflexion (verbal self-reflection) improves success rate on the HIRO self-correction category.

### Steps
1. **Baseline Measurement**: Run 50 trials without Reflexion, measuring the success rate on the HIRO self-correction category.
2. **Reflexion Integration**: Run 50 trials with Reflexion enabled, measuring the success rate on the same category.
3. **Comparison**: Compare the success rates from the two sets of trials to determine if Reflexion improves performance.

### What to Measure
- Success rate (number of correct responses out of total trials)
- Time taken per trial
- Number of self-reflection steps taken

### What Constitutes a Positive Result
A positive result is defined as a statistically significant improvement in success rate (p < 0.05) when Reflexion is enabled compared to the baseline.
