## The Story

You built a self-evolving AI agent on a $400 GPU. It doesn't evolve model weights (everyone does that) — it evolves its own harness (tools, memory, orchestration, security). The thesis: Metacognitive Harness Evolution (MHE) — three levers (parametric, contextual, structural), directed by a self-model that tracks which lever fixes which failure.

---

## The Arc — 7 Days, 1 Reel/Day

### Day 1: "Everyone's evolving AI wrong"
- **Hook:** Every self-evolving AI system fine-tunes model weights. What if that's not the lever that matters?
- **Core:** 3 levers — parametric, contextual, structural. Harness changes (+36pp from HAL paper) > most model upgrades.
- **CTA:** "I'm building Professor X — an agent that evolves its own code, not its weights. On a $400 GPU."

### Day 2: "This AI runs on a gaming PC, not a data center"
- **Hook:** SJTU built ASI-Evolve with H800 clusters. I built the same experiment on an RTX 3060.
- **Core:** Consumer hardware feasibility. If the harness is the dominant lever, you don't need frontier compute.
- **CTA:** "If harness evolution works, it should work on cheap hardware. That's the bet."

### Day 3: "I found a paper that almost killed my project"
- **Hook:** Day 3 of building Professor X. I found a paper called MOSS.
- **Core:** MOSS does source-level harness rewriting — the same thing. But it uses an external CLI, no metacognition, no identity coherence. Here's how Professor X is different.
- **CTA:** "Competition validates the space. Differentiation wins."

### Day 4: "Your AI agent doesn't know who it is"
- **Hook:** Every self-improving system gets better but loses itself.
- **Core:** The Strange Loop — Identity-Preserving Evolution (IPE). Behavioral fingerprinting. ICS score. Your agent should still be itself after 30 rounds of self-modification.
- **CTA:** "What if your AI could improve without losing its identity?"

### Day 5: "The 5-layer diagnostic that fixes itself"
- **Hook:** When your agent fails, which layer broke? Retrieval? Context? Tools? Reasoning?
- **Core:** DHE — Diagnostic Harness Engine. 5-layer probe, causal attribution, then the Researcher/Engineer/Analyzer loop fixes the right thing.
- **CTA:** "Most agents retry. Professor X diagnoses."

### Day 6: "The benchmark that doesn't exist yet"
- **Hook:** There's no benchmark for harness-level improvement. So we built one.
- **Core:** HIRO — isolates the harness contribution by freezing the model. 60 tasks, 4 baselines, the whole design.
- **CTA:** "If you can't measure it, you can't claim it."

### Day 7: "30 days, 30 rounds, $400 — the experiment"
- **Hook:** Week 1 recap. What we're running, what we're measuring, what we're betting.
- **Core:** Full picture. The 4-baseline experiment. The hypotheses. The paper outline.
- **CTA:** "Follow the build. 30 rounds. One GPU. We'll see if the harness is the dominant lever."
